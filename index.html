  • Activity

Activity

Dogs are very active animals. They need a lot of action and exercise. You should take your dog out at least two to three times a day for a walk or a short session of exercise. You need to increase to duration of these sessions by adding 5min more for each month of age in the beginning. It is important that your dog is enjoying the walks and exercises to see results. Allow him a little freedom to have fun and to play with other dogs. Keep looking for signs: Is he enjoying the time? Is he getting tired? Is he feeling uncomfortable with a certain exercise? Do not force him to activities he obviously does not like or the has not enough energy for.

Raising

Raising is one of the most important things for the dog owner and of course for the dog. It is necessary, that you have enough time and patience for your dog's training.
There are some differences in raising a puppy compared to an older dog. If you got your new friend after a while when he is not a puppy anymore, a lot of the raising is probably done. Your Dog should be trained already. He manages basic things like potty Training or common tricks, but you must continue with the Training. Try to teach more difficult things or combinations. You can try to get rid of bad habits your dog might have developed over the time or you can keep older achievements up to date.
If he is still very young you must pay attention to other things. Basic behaviour must be trained, and this can be difficult. If you are making mistakes your dog will keep these bad habits.
That’s why: be careful while rising a dog and keep paying attention to your dog’s behaviour. He will show you if he is feeling comfortable with new situations.
